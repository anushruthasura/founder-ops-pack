# Launch Command Center

Launch program management, sanitized from the live command board used for a
company-wide product launch - including, recursively, the launch this pack
shipped in.

## Rule 1: front-load the asks

Every ask of every human goes out WEEKS early, with the deadline attached -
not when the item becomes urgent. People deliver against deadlines they've
known about; they resent (and miss) deadlines that arrive pre-expired.
The board tracks every ask as: what / who / asked-on / due / status. An ask
without a due date is a wish; an ask made <72h before it's needed is a favor.

## Rule 2: evergreen over dead zones

Check the calendar for dead zones (holidays, industry events, summer
Fridays) BEFORE setting the date - and when a dead zone forces a move,
move LATER, never earlier-and-rushed. Prefer launch assets that stay true
for months (evergreen) over assets that expire at the event ("join us
Tuesday!") - the launch tail is where most of the compounding happens,
and expired assets amputate the tail.

## Rule 3: explicit first-hour engagement targets

Set numeric engagement targets for the first hour ON PAPER before launch
(e.g., N comments on the main post, M reshares/QRTs). Not because the
numbers are magic - because a numeric target converts "everyone please
support the launch" into a checkable result and makes the first hour a
JOB with owners, not a vibe. Algorithms weight early velocity; so do
humans deciding whether this thing is a thing.

## Rule 4: the launch never ends

The launch date is the midpoint, not the finish. The board's cadence
continues after launch day: what shipped in the launch grows in waves
(and says so publicly - "lands in waves through launch week" beats
silence when wave 2 is late). The post-launch weeks get the same
tracker discipline as the pre-launch weeks; most teams stop tracking
exactly when the compounding starts.

## The board (single source of truth)

One board, visible to everyone involved:
- **Countdown & date rationale** (why THIS date, what dead zones were dodged)
- **Asks tracker** (what/who/asked/due/status - the front-loading engine)
- **Asset manifest** (each asset: owner, evergreen-or-dated, status, link)
- **First-hour plan** (targets, who posts what when, who engages, trigger words)
- **Day-by-day execution** (T-7 through T+7 minimum)
- **Decision log** (dated: what changed and why - launches accrete verbal
  decisions that contradict each other unless written)

## If/then

- If an ask is slipping (due <72h, status not started) -> escalate to the
  owner directly today; the board is for tracking, the nudge is human.
- If the date must move -> move ONCE, announce the new date with the reason,
  and re-date every downstream ask the same day. Serial small slips burn
  more credibility than one honest big one.
- If an asset won't be ready -> cut it from day-one and add it to a wave;
  never launch a placeholder. "Coming in wave 2" is a promise; a broken
  link is a verdict.
- If first-hour targets are missed by >50% -> run the contingency NOW
  (owner DMs to the amplifier list), not a retro next week.

## Escalation

- Two asks from the same person slipping -> the problem is their bandwidth,
  not their memory; re-assign or de-scope with them directly.
- Legal/brand/security review not started at T-7 -> flag to leadership;
  reviews are the classic invisible dead zone.

## Worked example

Product launch, target Wednesday July 1. Dead-zone check: July 4th week -
US audience gone Thursday onward, press dead. Decision (logged): move to
Wednesday July 8 - LATER, not rushed-earlier; Wednesday preserved as the
optimal B2B day. Asks front-loaded 3 weeks out: 14 asks with dues (exec
amplification, customer quotes x3, demo video, blog draft, partner posts).
T-10: quote ask #2 slipping -> direct nudge, delivered T-8. First-hour plan
on paper: 300 comments / 100 reshares hour one; trigger word for the
giveaway comment mechanic; 12 owners with assigned actions in the first
60 minutes; amplifier DM list of 17 named people. Asset manifest: 9 assets,
7 evergreen; the 2 dated ones (day-of posts) owned separately. T+1 through
T+7: wave-2 assets land per the public "waves" note; board cadence continues
daily. Decision log: 11 entries by launch, including the date move -
zero relitigated decisions.

## Definition of done

- [ ] Every ask has what/who/asked/due/status; none issued <72h before need
- [ ] Date checked against dead zones; move (if any) was later, once, logged
- [ ] First-hour targets numeric, on paper, with named owners
- [ ] Asset manifest marks evergreen vs dated; no placeholders at launch
- [ ] Decision log current; post-launch cadence scheduled BEFORE launch day
- [ ] Amplifier list built with owners for the first hour

## Anti-patterns

- **Just-in-time asks.** The T-2 "quick favor" is how launch weeks acquire
  their reputation. Front-loading is a kindness that reads as competence.
- **Date-stamped everything.** When every asset says "today we're announcing",
  the launch has a 24-hour shelf life. The tail is where the compounding is;
  evergreen assets are how you keep it.
- **Vibes-based first hours.** "Everyone amplify!" produces 40 likes from the
  same 12 teammates. Named owners with assigned actions and numeric targets
  produce a first hour that algorithms and onlookers both register.
- **Stopping the board at T+1.** The retro-then-silence pattern wastes the
  most receptive audience you'll ever have: people who just noticed you.

## Setup (T-minus 3+ weeks, ~2 hours)

1. Stand up the board with all six sections, even if some start thin.
2. Dead-zone pass on the calendar BEFORE announcing any date internally -
   moving a date everyone planned around costs 10x moving it on paper.
3. Write the asks list in one sitting (they're all knowable at T-3w),
   then issue them all with dues. The discomfort of asking early is the
   feature.
4. Draft the first-hour plan with names attached; get each owner's explicit
   yes. An owner who found out from the board isn't an owner.
5. Schedule the T+1..T+7 board reviews NOW - post-launch cadence decided
   before launch is cadence that survives launch-day exhaustion.

## Swappable parameters

- First-hour targets: `300 comments / 100 reshares` -> your audience size
- Front-load horizon: `3 weeks` -> your org's ask-latency
- Slip escalation: `<72h + not started` -> your risk tolerance
- Wave window: `T+7` -> your content pipeline depth
