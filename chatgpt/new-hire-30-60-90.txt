# New Hire 30/60/90

The plan is a mutual action plan between manager and hire: named
outcomes, dated check-ins, and an audit trail where nothing is ever
deleted, only struck through with a reason.

## The three horizons

- **Day 30, learning:** can they explain the system, the customers, and
  who owns what? Outcome: a written artifact (their map of the territory)
  reviewed by the manager. Understanding is verified in writing, not vibes.
- **Day 60, contributing:** first owned deliverable shipped, small
  enough to finish, real enough to matter.
- **Day 90, owning:** an area where they are the named owner and others
  route to them.

Each horizon: 3-5 outcomes, each with a named verifier. Goals accumulate
long-term → mid-term → immediate; a day-90 goal appears at day 0 and
sharpens, it does not materialize at day 75.

## The accounting rules

- Never delete a goal. Changed or dropped goals get struck through with
  a dated reason. The strike-through history IS the onboarding record.
- Every row carries a last-updated date.
- Check-in dates: the soonest next step is always dated tomorrow at the
  earliest, never today. "Today" items are already late items in denial.

## If/then rules

- If a day-30 outcome slips → adjust days 60/90 in the same edit. A
  slipped foundation with unmoved later goals is a plan lying to itself.
- If 2+ outcomes in one horizon get struck → the role's scope was
  misdrawn; manager rewrites the horizon and says so to the hire.
- If a check-in is skipped → it is rescheduled within 2 business days,
  not folded into the next one. Two check-ins of feedback in one sitting
  overloads exactly the person with the least context.

## Worked example (real math)

Support-lead hire. Day-30 check-in grades 4 outcomes: 3 verified, but
"shadow 10 customer calls" sits at 6 of 10 because call volume dropped.
The goal is struck through with the dated reason and replaced by "6
shadows + 4 recorded-call reviews, by day 40". Updating it means
updating ALL affected fields together: the strike-through with its
reason, the replacement row with its new date and verifier, the day-60
outcome that depended on it ("run 5 calls solo" moves from day 60 to
day 70), and both last-updated stamps. At day 90 the plan shows 11
outcomes verified, 2 struck with reasons, and the strike history reads
as an honest record of the ramp, which is exactly what the 6-month
review will want.

## Validation (definition of done)

- [ ] Every outcome has a named verifier and a written artifact where applicable
- [ ] All three horizons populated at day 0, sharpening over time
- [ ] Zero deletions; every change is a dated strike-through with a reason
- [ ] Slips cascade to dependent goals in the same edit
- [ ] Next check-in always dated tomorrow or later

## Adapting this to your company

Swap: the horizon lengths, outcomes per horizon. Keep: named verifiers,
strike-through accounting, cascading edits, and never-today dating.
