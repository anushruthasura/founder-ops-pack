# Feedback (SBI)

Feedback is testimony, not diagnosis. Report what happened and what it
cost; the recipient owns what it means about them.

## The three parts

1. **Situation:** one specific time and place. "In Tuesday's pricing
   review" not "lately".
2. **Behavior:** what was observably said or done, verbatim where
   possible. If you would not say it with the recording playing, it is
   interpretation, not behavior. "You said 'this is a waste of time'"
   qualifies; "you were dismissive" does not.
3. **Impact:** the concrete effect, with a number or a name. "We lost
   20 minutes and two people messaged me after" beats "it hurt morale".

## The rules

- Within 48 hours of the event. Week-old feedback is archaeology; the
  details both parties need are gone.
- One event per conversation. Three stacked examples is an ambush.
- Adjective test before delivering: every adjective in the draft gets
  replaced with the observable it stands on, or cut.

## If/then rules

- If you cannot name the specific situation → you do not have feedback
  yet, you have a mood. Wait for the next instance.
- If the behavior sentence contains "always" or "never" → rewrite to the
  single instance. Patterns are for the third conversation, with dates.
- If the impact is only "it bothered me" → deliver it as a preference,
  not as feedback, and say so.
- If it is a pattern (3+ logged instances) → bring the dated instances,
  and the conversation is about the pattern, stated as one.

## Worked example (real math)

Draft: "You were unprepared and it derailed the demo." Adjective test:
"unprepared" stands on what? Rewritten: Situation: "Thursday's demo for
the Acme renewal." Behavior: "The environment wasn't loaded; you said
'give me five minutes' and set up while they waited." Impact: "We used
19 of our 45 minutes on setup, and their VP left before the Q&A."
Delivered Friday, inside 48 hours. Logging it means updating ALL
affected fields together: the 1:1 notes gain the SBI entry with its
date, and the follow-up ("dry-run checklist before customer demos")
lands on the task list with an owner and next demo's date. A feedback
conversation that produces no logged follow-up is a vent with structure.

## Validation (definition of done)

- [ ] Situation names one time and place
- [ ] Behavior is verbatim or observable, zero adjectives
- [ ] Impact carries a number or a name
- [ ] Delivered within 48 hours, one event only
- [ ] Follow-up logged with an owner and a date

## Adapting this to your company

Swap: the 48-hour window, the 3-instance pattern bar. Keep: the
adjective test, verbatim behavior, and concrete impact.
