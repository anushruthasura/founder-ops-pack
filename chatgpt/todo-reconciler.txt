# Todo Reconciler

Most task lists die the same way: items get added faithfully and removed
never, because **most todos resolve out-of-band** — you answer the email,
the decision happens in a meeting, someone else ships the fix. An
append-only list drifts from reality within days, you stop trusting it,
and you stop looking at it.

The fix is architectural, not motivational: **reconcile, don't append.**

## Rule 1: Every refresh re-reads the sources

On every refresh, re-read the actual source systems — inbox, chat channels,
meeting notes, ticket tracker — and re-derive the list:

- An open item whose underlying thread got a reply from you → **auto-close**.
- An item whose deliverable shipped (PR merged, doc sent, invite accepted)
  → **auto-close**, with a one-line note of the evidence.
- New actionable things found in sources → **surface as new items**.
- Everything else → carries forward, with its age incremented.

Never trust the list's memory over the sources. The list is a *view* over
the sources, not a database of record.

## Rule 2: Group items into Situations, and re-judge at the Situation level

A **Situation** is an entity-anchored group — "the Acme renewal", "hiring
the ops lead", "the Q3 board meeting" — whose truth is spread across email
AND chat AND meeting notes AND documents at once.

Reconcile per Situation, not per item, because **a decision landing in one
source obsoletes items sourced from another.** If the renewal call
transcript says "they signed", the todo that came from an email thread
("chase Acme signature") is dead — even though nothing in email says so.
Item-level reconciliation can never catch this; Situation-level re-judging
catches it every time.

Practical Situation sizing: 5–15 active Situations. More than ~20 means
you're creating a Situation per item (pointless); fewer than 3 means
they're too broad to re-judge in one look.

## Rule 3: Staleness is itself a triage trigger

Age isn't decoration — it forces decisions:

- Item untouched **7 days**: the refresh flags it with an explicit
  question: still real?
- Item untouched **14 days**: it must be actively dispositioned —
  **keep** (with a reason written down), **dismiss** (consciously not
  doing it), or **done** (it resolved and reconciliation missed the
  evidence — also note WHY it was missed, that's a source gap).
- Nothing lives past 14 days undecided. An item you refuse to decide on
  for two weeks is not a task, it's guilt. Dismiss is a first-class
  outcome, not a failure.

## Rule 4: Time the full reconcile so the list is trustworthy at day start

Two refresh cadences:
- **Light refresh** (hourly or on-demand): incremental — new items in,
  obvious completions out.
- **Full reconcile** (once daily, BEFORE the workday — e.g. 6am): re-read
  everything, re-judge every Situation, apply staleness triage.

The full reconcile runs before the day starts for one reason: the list's
value is entirely a function of whether you trust it at 8am. A list
reconciled at 6am gets used; a list reconciled at noon gets second-guessed
all morning.

## Worked example: one Situation through a reconcile

Situation: **"Q3 vendor contract renewal"** (anchored on vendor: Meridian).
Items before the 6am reconcile:
1. "Reply to Meridian pricing email" (from email, 3 days old)
2. "Get legal read on liability clause" (from chat, 9 days old — flagged)
3. "Schedule renewal call" (from meeting notes, 1 day old)

Reconcile re-reads sources and finds: yesterday's leadership meeting
transcript contains "we're consolidating vendors — Meridian isn't making
the cut."

Result: the WHOLE Situation re-judges to wind-down. Item 1 rewrites to
"send Meridian a decline note", items 2 and 3 auto-close as obsolete
(evidence: leadership meeting decision), and the Situation gets a 7-day
countdown to archive. An item-level reconciler would have kept all three
alive — nothing in email or chat said anything changed.

## Validation (definition of done for a refresh)

A refresh is correct only if:
1. Every auto-closed item has one line of evidence (the reply, the merge,
   the transcript quote) — auto-close without evidence is deletion.
2. Every item still open after the refresh exists in at least one source —
   orphaned items (source deleted/archived) get flagged, not silently kept.
3. Every item past its staleness threshold carries an explicit disposition
   or a today-dated flag. Zero silent 14-day-old items.
4. The refresh reports its own numbers: N found, N closed, N new,
   N flagged. If closed = 0 across several refreshes, reconciliation is
   broken (real life always closes items out-of-band) — treat it as a bug
   in source coverage, not as an unusually diligent week.

## Adapting this to your stack

Sources are swappable: any inbox, any chat tool, any notes system, any
ticket tracker. The four rules are not: re-read sources, re-judge per
Situation, staleness forces triage, full reconcile before the day starts.
