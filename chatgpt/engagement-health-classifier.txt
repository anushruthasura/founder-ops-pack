# Engagement Health Classifier

CSM book-of-business review as a classifier, sanitized from a production
customer-success tracker. Two design decisions do all the work: exactly
three states, and matching that catches what naive joins miss.

## Rule 1: three states, because three forces a decision

- **Healthy** - engaged and trending flat-or-up. Action: nothing. (A state
  whose action is "nothing" is what makes the other two states actionable.)
- **Soft Nudge** - alive but cooling. Action: one light, personal, non-salesy
  touch within the week.
- **Reengage** - signal lost. Action: a deliberate re-engagement play with an
  owner and a date - not another newsletter.

Five-state models and 0-100 health scores produce discussion; three states
produce a to-do list. If you're debating between two adjacent states for
more than a minute, pick the worse one (ties break toward action).

## Rule 2: 3-way meeting-to-account matching

Meetings are your richest engagement signal, and the easiest to lose:
attendees use personal emails constantly. Match calendar meetings to
accounts three ways, taking any hit:

1. **Owner domain** - any attendee's email domain matches the account domain.
2. **Name token** - account name (or a known alias) appears in the meeting
   title or description.
3. **Attendee membership** - an attendee is already a known contact of the
   account, regardless of the address they used today.

Match 3 is the one that pays: the champion who books from gmail is EXACTLY
the meeting you can't afford to count as no-meeting. In production, adding
matches 2+3 reclassified accounts from Reengage to Healthy - accounts that
were being nudged while actively meeting with us.

## Rule 3: the honest trend is 7d vs prior 7d

Usage trend = rolling last-7-days vs the 7 days before that. Not month-over-
month (too slow to catch a cliff), not day-over-day (all noise). The 7v7
comparison is the shortest window that survives weekends and the longest
one that still catches a cooling account before it's cold.

## Classification logic

For each engagement, gather: meetings (3-way matched, last 14 days),
usage 7v7 trend, last genuine human contact (reply/call - not opens).
Then: recent meeting OR rising usage -> **Healthy**. No meeting in 14d but
usage flat and a human contact in 21d -> **Soft Nudge**. Usage falling
(>30% drop 7v7) OR no human contact in 21+ days -> **Reengage**, regardless
of anything else looking fine.

## If/then

- If states disagree with your gut -> the data is missing a source (usually
  a support thread or a champion's personal-email meeting). Fix the matching,
  don't override the state; overrides are how classifiers become decoration.
- If an account sits in Soft Nudge 3 consecutive runs -> it's not soft
  anymore; promote to Reengage. Soft Nudge is a transit state, not a home.
- If a Reengage play gets no response in 14 days -> escalate to owner with
  the full timeline; silence after a deliberate play is churn talking.
- If >30% of the book is Reengage -> the problem is upstream (onboarding,
  product, pricing), not the CSM motion. Escalate the pattern, not 30 accounts.

## Escalation

- Healthy -> Reengage in a single run (skipping Soft Nudge) -> same-day owner
  alert: cliffs have causes (champion left, budget cut, incident) and the
  first 48 hours are when asking still works.
- Any account with an open renewal inside 90 days classified Reengage ->
  standing weekly review until resolved.

## Worked example

Monday run, 24 accounts. Acme: no owner-domain meetings in 14d - but match 3
catches Thursday's "sync w/ Dave" booked from dave.k@gmail.com (Dave is a
known Acme contact). Usage 7v7: +12%. -> Healthy (was headed for Reengage
under naive matching; this is the 3-way match paying rent). BetaCo: usage
7v7 -34%, last human contact 26 days -> Reengage; play assigned (owner: you;
"champion check-in call, not email"; due Wed). Gamma: flat usage, no meetings,
human reply 11 days ago -> Soft Nudge, third consecutive -> promoted to
Reengage per the transit rule. Output: 17 Healthy (untouched), 4 Soft Nudge
(one personal touch each), 3 Reengage (each with play + owner + date).
Review took 20 minutes and produced 7 actions.

## Definition of done

- [ ] Every account in exactly one state; no "watch" pile, no overrides
- [ ] Meeting matching ran all 3 ways; personal-email meetings counted
- [ ] Trend is 7d vs prior 7d from genuine usage (auto-noise excluded)
- [ ] Every Soft Nudge has a touch; every Reengage has play + owner + date
- [ ] State changes since last run reviewed, cliffs escalated same-day

## Anti-patterns

- **The "watch list" fourth state.** Watch is where decisions go to hide.
  Every account you'd put on watch is a Soft Nudge (do the touch) or a
  Reengage (run the play). The third state you actually need is "Healthy -
  do nothing", and it takes discipline to leave it alone.
- **Health scores with decimals.** A 67 vs a 71 produces a conversation about
  the model. Three states produce a conversation about the customer.
- **Owner-domain-only matching.** The naive join undercounts engagement for
  exactly your most engaged humans (champions book from phones and personal
  accounts). If your Reengage list contains an account you talked to this
  week, your matching is broken - fix it before trusting anything else.
- **Manual state overrides.** Overriding states without fixing the underlying
  data source turns the classifier into decoration within a month.

## Setup (first run, ~45 minutes)

1. List accounts with: domain(s), known contacts (name + all known emails),
   aliases/nicknames for the name-token match.
2. Wire the three signals: calendar (14d window), usage (7v7), last human
   contact (21d). Missing one? Run with two and say so on the output -
   a classifier with named gaps beats one with silent ones.
3. Run once, then hand-audit the 5 most surprising classifications. Each
   surprise is a matching gap or a missing source; fix, re-run.
4. Book the recurring run + the 20-minute action review as one calendar
   block. The classification is worthless without the actions half.

## Swappable parameters

- Windows: meetings `14d`, human contact `21d`, cliff `>30% 7v7` -> your product's tempo
- Soft-Nudge transit: `3 runs` -> your cadence
- Book-level alarm: `>30% Reengage` -> your baseline
- Cadence: weekly -> your book size (daily above ~50 accounts changes nothing but noise)
