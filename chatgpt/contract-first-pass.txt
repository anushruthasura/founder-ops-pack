# Contract First Pass

The first pass is triage, not judgment. It exists so counsel time (at
$400-700/hr) is spent on the 5 flagged clauses, not on locating them.
This skill prepares questions. It never answers them.

## The fixed checklist (every contract, same order)

| Clause | Extract | Flag when |
|---|---|---|
| Term | Start, length, renewal type | Initial term > 12 months |
| Auto-renewal | Notice window and mechanics | Notice > 30 days, or renewal term > initial term |
| Price escalators | Any % or "then-current pricing" | Uncapped, or > 7%/yr |
| Liability cap | Cap amount and carve-outs | Uncapped exposure, or cap < fees paid where you carry the risk |
| Data terms | Ownership, deletion, subprocessors | Vendor claims derived-data rights, or no deletion-on-exit clause |
| Termination | For-convenience rights, cure periods | Only they can terminate for convenience |
| Payment | Net terms, late fees | Net < 15, or late fee > 1.5%/mo |
| Assignment | Change-of-control language | Consent required from only one side |

Every extraction carries its section number. A flag without a pin costs
counsel ten minutes of scrolling.

## The renewal-trap arithmetic

Auto-renew plus a long notice window is a delayed decision disguised as
convenience. Always compute the real decision date: renewal date minus
notice window, then subtract 30 more days for internal review. That date
goes on the calendar the day the contract is signed.

## If/then rules

- If the contract references an external document ("per the SLA at the
  URL") → fetch and version-stamp it. Unfetched references are unsigned
  blank pages.
- If any single flag involves uncapped liability, IP assignment, or
  exclusivity → the whole contract escalates to counsel before further
  negotiation, not after.
- If the counterparty sends a redline → diff against the last version and
  first-pass only the changed clauses. Never re-review from scratch; a
  changed comma hides in a full re-read.
- If the same vendor's paper flags the same clause twice in a year →
  push your own template for the next renewal.

## Worked example (real math)

A $36,000/yr platform MSA, 24-month initial term. The pass extracts:
auto-renewal with 90-day notice (section 11.2), renewal at "then-current
list pricing" (11.3), liability capped at 6 months of fees (14.1), no
deletion-on-exit clause in the data addendum. Four flags: term > 12
months, notice > 30 days, uncapped escalator, missing deletion clause.
The renewal-trap math: renewal lands June 30, 2028, minus 90 days notice
is April 1, minus 30 review days puts the real decision date at March 2,
2028. Logging the contract means updating ALL affected fields together:
the flag list to counsel with section pins, the calendar entry on
March 2, the vendor row in the stack audit with the notice window, and
the budget line noting the uncapped escalator risk. A flagged escalator
that never reaches the budget file becomes next year's surprise.

## Escalation

Output is always "flags for counsel", ranked by exposure, each with a
section pin and a one-line why. The three auto-escalate clauses
(uncapped liability, IP assignment, exclusivity) go to counsel same-day.
Nothing in the pass output is worded as a recommendation to sign.

## Validation (definition of done)

- [ ] All 8 checklist rows extracted with section numbers
- [ ] Real decision date computed and on the calendar
- [ ] Every flag has a section pin and a one-line why
- [ ] Auto-escalate clauses (if any) sent to counsel same-day
- [ ] Redlines diffed, only changed clauses re-passed
- [ ] Output framed as questions for counsel, zero legal conclusions

## Adapting this to your company

Swap: the flag thresholds (12 months, 30 days, 7%), the checklist extras
for your industry. Keep: fixed order, section pins, the decision-date
arithmetic, and the counsel-questions-only framing.
