# Bug Sev Triage

Severity is defined by user impact, decided once at intake, by
definition rather than by whoever shouts. The out-of-scope list is what
keeps the queue honest.

## The out-of-scope list (the secret)

Written down, published, applied at intake. Not bugs: infra blips that
auto-recovered, config mistakes on the user's side, known platform
latency within stated bounds, feature requests wearing a bug costume.
Each gets routed to its actual home, not sev-4ed into a queue where it
rots. Expect the list to absorb 30-50% of incoming reports.

## Severity definitions and clocks

- **Sev-1:** data loss, security exposure, or all users blocked.
  Response now, all hands until mitigated, then a written postmortem.
- **Sev-2:** a core workflow broken with no workaround. Response same
  business day, fix inside the current cycle.
- **Sev-3:** broken with a workaround, or a non-core path. Next cycle,
  batched.
- **Sev-4:** cosmetic. Fixed opportunistically; auto-closed with a note
  after 90 days untouched.

## If/then rules

- If a report matches the out-of-scope list → route it out at intake
  with one line; it never enters the sev queue.
- If severity is disputed → the definitions decide, not seniority. A
  workaround existing caps it at sev-3 regardless of who filed it.
- If the same root cause generates 3+ reports in a week → the cluster
  escalates one sev level; frequency is impact.
- If a sev-2 misses its cycle → it is stated in the cycle review by
  name, with its new date. Silent slips are how sev-2 becomes sev-3
  culturally.

## Worked example (real math)

Monday intake: 14 reports. Out-of-scope list absorbs 6 (43%, inside the
30-50% band): 2 config, 3 auto-recovered blips, 1 feature request
rerouted to the PRD backlog. Remaining 8: one export failure with no
workaround lands sev-2 with a same-day response; five sev-3s; two
cosmetic sev-4s. Wednesday, two more export-failure reports arrive,
same root cause: 3 reports in a week trips the cluster rule, and
escalating means updating ALL affected fields together: the sev field
(2 → 1), the response clock (all-hands now), the linked duplicate
reports pointing at the parent, and the status page note. An escalated
parent with orphaned duplicates gets triaged twice by two people.

## Validation (definition of done)

- [ ] Out-of-scope list published and applied at intake, absorption rate in band
- [ ] Every queued bug has a sev assigned by definition, at intake
- [ ] All sev-1/sev-2 response clocks met or their misses named in review
- [ ] Duplicate reports linked to a parent; clusters checked weekly
- [ ] Sev-4s auto-closed at 90 days with notes

## Adapting this to your company

Swap: the clock durations, the 90-day auto-close, the cluster threshold.
Keep: the out-of-scope list, definition-decided severity, workaround
caps, and cluster escalation.
